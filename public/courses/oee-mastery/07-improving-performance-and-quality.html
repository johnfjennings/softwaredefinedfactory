Improving Performance & Quality

Tackling the hidden losses in speed and yield

Software Defined Factory

Performance: The Hidden Giant

Performance losses are the most commonly overlooked OEE losses.

Unlike breakdowns (everyone notices) or defects (quality catches them), speed losses happen silently.

  • Minor stops: 30-second jams that nobody records
  • Speed reductions: Running at 80% of ideal speed "because it's always been that way"

Tackling Minor Stops

Minor stops are typically under 5 minutes each, but they happen frequently.

Step 1: Make them visible
  • Install an andon light or alarm for any stop over 30 seconds
  • Automated stop logging captures what operators miss
  • Track frequency and duration separately

Minor Stop Root Causes

Symptom Common Root Cause Solution
Material jams Worn guides, incorrect material specs Replace guides, verify material specifications
Sensor trips Dirty sensors, misalignment Cleaning schedule, alignment verification
Misfeeds Hopper design, material clumping Feeder modifications, material handling improvements
Overloads Accumulated debris, wear Regular cleaning, component replacement schedule

Tackling Speed Losses

If your equipment runs below its rated speed, ask:

  1. Do we actually know the ideal cycle time for this product?
  2. Was the speed reduced to "fix" a quality or jam problem?
  3. Is the speed limited by upstream/downstream bottlenecks?
  4. Is there equipment wear causing vibration at higher speeds?
  5. Is operator training adequate for running at full speed?

Speed Recovery Process

  1. Verify nameplate speed — Check the equipment manual for designed capacity
  2. Run a trial at ideal speed — Observe what happens: jams? defects? vibration?
  3. Fix the barrier — Address the root cause that prevents full speed
  4. Increase gradually — Step up speed 5% at a time, verify stability at each level
  5. Standardize — Document the proven speed in the work instruction

Typical result: 5-15% speed improvement once root causes are addressed.

Quality: Getting to 99%+

Quality losses seem small in percentage terms, but they're expensive:

  • Every defect wastes machine time (not just material)
  • Rework consumes double the resources
  • Escaped defects damage customer trust

World-class quality is 99%+ first-pass yield.

Statistical Process Control (SPC)

SPC uses control charts to detect process drift before defects occur.

  • X-bar and R charts — Track process mean and variation
  • Upper/Lower Control Limits (UCL/LCL) — Statistical boundaries, not specification limits
  • Out-of-control signals — Point outside limits, 7 consecutive points trending, etc.
Key principle: Control limits are NOT the same as specification limits. A process can be in-spec but out-of-control (drifting toward failure).

Error-Proofing (Poka-Yoke)

Design processes so that mistakes are impossible or immediately detected.

Type Example Effectiveness
Prevention Asymmetric connectors that only fit one way Defect is impossible
Detection Sensor that stops the line if part is missing Defect caught immediately
Warning Alarm when parameter exceeds threshold Operator intervention required

Best poka-yoke: prevention. The defect literally cannot happen.

Reducing Startup Losses

Startup losses occur when the process produces defects during warm-up or after changeover.

  • Standardize startup parameters — Document the exact settings (temperature, pressure, speed) for each product
  • Pre-heat equipment — Start warm-up while the previous batch finishes (external task per SMED)
  • First-article inspection — Check the first 3-5 parts before running at full speed
  • Golden samples — Keep reference parts for visual comparison during startup

Combining the Approaches

The most effective quality strategy layers multiple techniques:

  1. SPC — Detects drift before specs are violated
  2. Poka-Yoke — Prevents or catches errors in real time
  3. 5-Why — Finds root causes of defects that do occur
  4. Standardized work — Ensures consistent execution shift to shift

Together, these techniques close the gap between 95% and 99%+ quality.

Key Takeaways

  • Performance losses are the most hidden — make them visible first
  • Know your true ideal cycle time before trying to improve speed
  • Minor stops add up — ten 2-minute stops = 20 minutes lost
  • SPC catches drift before it becomes defects
  • Poka-yoke is the ultimate quality tool: prevent, don't just detect
  • Standardize startup procedures to eliminate yield losses
